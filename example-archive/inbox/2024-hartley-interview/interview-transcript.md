# Interview with Ethel Hartley - transcript

*Recorded by phone, March 2024. Fictional fixture for the example archive. This is
the transcript half of a bundle stub (see notes.md beside it); the audio recording

**Q: Tell me about the house in Fairview.**

A: Oh, the house on Plains Street. My father Thomas kept the books for the railroad -
the Plains Junction line - and we were close enough to hear the yard at night. There
were three of us children that lived: Calvin, Frances, and me.

**Q: What do you remember about your father's lodge?**

A: He was a Mason. The Fairview lodge. He'd go down on a Tuesday and come back late.
There was a Mr. Layng he was friendly with from there - Charley, I think. They were
thick as thieves.

**Q: Do you remember when your parents married?**

A: Before my time, of course. Around the turn of the century I always thought, but
the family Bible said earlier - the eighteen-seventies. I was never sure.
